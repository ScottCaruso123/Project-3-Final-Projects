Know what you use!

Both energy companies and consumers benefit from conserving energy. Whether the motivations for energy conservation are economic, environmental, or personal, the energy company or consumer must first understand patterns of energy usage to take the best conservation approach. E-Predict uses historical data and forecasts energy consumption from the current date and time for any specified number of hours into the future. The ability to predict energy consumption based on historical hourly data allows users to strategically focus conservation efforts on the most impactful opportunities and prioritize key business objectives.

 


About the Project


Machine Learning for Predicting Industrial Hourly Energy Consumption


Our mission for this project was to create an easy to use tool to predict upcoming hourly energy consumption. Our analysis will help the energy provider company better understand their historical trends, and for the energy consumer, this app will be incredibly helpful in understanding energy consumption trends and when peak pricing will kick in.

The Data & Model


The Data

We used 14 years of electric energy data and piloted our model with one of the nation’s largest electricity producers, American Electric Power. American Electric Power (AEP) is a major investor-owned electric utility in the United States of America, delivering electricity to more than five million customers in 11 states.


In these four charts we explore the long and short term trends in our data. We observe a spike in 2007 and fairly steady decline after this. The week of the year chart displays how seasonality affects our data. It appears that there is local minimum in the spring and fall. Our day of the week chart visualizes the difference in energy demands from weekdays and weekends, where the weekends are statistically significant lower than the weekday. Finally, we can observe a noticeable trend in the hours of the day where the hours of 1 AM to 6 AM have the lowest energy consumption. From 6 AM there is steady growth until Noon, where growth slows down and holds steady until 1 AM.

The Model

E-Predict is based on the XGBoost model. XGBoost stands for extreme gradient boosting, a regression technique utilizing decision trees. The XGBoost model is designed for speed and model performance. XGBoost models “dominate on classification and regression predictive modeling problems”. This model uses day of year and hour for energy prediction demands. The model was piloted with 14 years of energy demand data from one of the nation's largest electricity generators, American Electric Power (AEP).
